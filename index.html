Use the UI widgets to define the scene and the mouse to rotate/zoom/move it.

Implementation Notes

  • Only the edges used in the construction of the polyhedra and their faces are displayed. The implicit edges at intersecting faces are omitted. As a consequence, the great dodecahedron shows the same edges as the plain icosahedron. The same holds for their transformed versions.
  • While the dodecahedron and its fully transformed variant have planar faces (pentagons or pentagrams), the faces of the intermediate polyhedra are not planar. Thus there is no canonical way to draw these faces. My solution is to triangulate a pentagon around its center, actually the gravicenter of the 5 edges. (Using the edge center instead of the vertex center has the advantage that it fits with the "intuitive" center even when two vertices coincide, degenerating the pentagon to a quadrangle.)

Geometry Notes

  • Linear interpolation between φ and -1/φ with parameters (1 - 1 / √5) / 2 ≈ 0.276 and (1 + 1 / √5) / 2 ≈ 0.724 yields 1 and 0. This makes certain vertices collapse and the figures get (almost) cubic/octahedral symmetry.
  • The soccerball is constructed from the icosahedron by cutting off 1/3 of each end of each edge and by connecting the 5 new vertices around each original vertex.
    I have made the truncation configurable (between 0 and 1/2) so that there is a continuous transition icosahedron–soccerball–icosidodecahedron.
    Hint: With the fully transformed icosahedron vary the truncation between 0 and 1/3 to see truncation of a great icosahedron.
